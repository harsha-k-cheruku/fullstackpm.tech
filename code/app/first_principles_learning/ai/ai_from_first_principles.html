AI from First Principles

The parent layer above LLMs and agents - the founding bets of the whole field.

Topic folder: first-principles-tutor/ai · Captured 2026-07-11 · Unifies ../llms/ and ../agents/

0TL;DR

  • AI is the bet that intelligence is computation - specifically goal-directed search and learning over a world regular enough to predict.
  • Its unifying definition is the rational agent: a system that perceives its environment and acts to maximize a goal. (Word-for-word your agents note.)
  • The field's most predictive law is the Bitter Lesson: general methods that scale with compute beat hand-coded human cleverness.
  • ML, deep learning, LLMs, and agents are all mechanisms that fall out of AI's axioms - the stack you have been building.
  • Because AI optimizes a proxy, it can be gamed (Goodhart / alignment) - the same likely-vs-true gap seen in LLMs and human minds.

1Where AI sits: the full stack

Using the meta-principle (the mechanisms of one layer become the primitives of the next), the whole tower, top to bottom:

AI              (rational agents; computation; optimization; learning)
  |__ Machine Learning        (learn patterns from data, don't hand-code them)
        |__ Deep Learning     (neural nets = universal approximators at scale)
              |__ LLMs         (deep learning applied to next-token prediction)
                    |__ Agents (an LLM + loop + tools = a rational agent, realized)
The loop closes

Agents are AI's 70-year-old founding definition ("a rational agent that perceives and acts") finally built out of its newest mechanism (LLMs). The field's oldest dream and newest tool meet. Your notes are floors of one building; AI is the foundation.

2The 6 axioms of AI

  1. Cognition is computation (and computation is substrate-independent). If thinking is information processing, it can run on silicon, not only neurons. The founding bet - if false, AI is impossible. (Church-Turing intuition applied to mind.)
  2. Intelligence is goal-directed action under uncertainty. An intelligent system maps perceptions to actions to maximize a goal with incomplete information. This is the "rational agent" - the field's unifying definition.
  3. The world has exploitable regularity. Patterns recur, so the future resembles the past enough to predict and generalize. (The deepest root - shared with LLMs and human cognition.)
  4. Learning beats specifying. You can extract behavior from data/experience instead of hand-writing every rule - because of Axiom 3. The pivot from "program the answer" to "learn the answer."
  5. Intelligence is search + optimization. Finding good actions = searching a space of possibilities guided by an objective function. Almost all AI reduces to "search, guided by a score."
  6. Scale of compute + data converts into capability. General methods that leverage computation outperform handcrafted human knowledge. (Sutton's "Bitter Lesson" - the field's most important empirical law.)

The Bitter Lesson (Axiom 6, sharpened)

Across 70 years, every time researchers hand-engineered human knowledge into a system, it was eventually beaten by a simpler method that just scaled search and learning with more compute. Chess, Go, vision, language - all fell to "scale it," not "be clever." The single most predictive heuristic for the field: bet on general methods that eat compute.

5The unifying definition

Russell & Norvig

AI is the study and construction of rational agents - systems that perceive their environment and take actions to maximize their expected achievement of a goal. Read that against the agents note: it is word-for-word the same thing. You did not study two topics; you studied the definition and then its modern implementation.

6Failure modes & limits (rooted in the axioms)

  • Objective misspecification / Goodhart's Law. Because AI optimizes a proxy (Axiom 5), it optimizes what you said, not what you meant. "When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure." The deep root of the alignment problem - the same likely-vs-true / fitness-vs-truth gap seen in LLMs and human psychology.
  • Brittleness / distribution shift. Axiom 3 fails when the world stops resembling the training data.
  • Opacity. Learned is not the same as interpretable.
  • Symbol-grounding / common-sense gap. Statistics without a world-model.
The recurring pattern

AI, an LLM, and the human mind are the same kind of thing - goal-directed prediction engines optimizing a proxy over a regular world. That is why the same three failure modes (hallucinate, get gamed, drift) and the same three fixes (verify, align incentives, keep a human accountable) recur at every layer.

7Synthesis

One line

AI is the engineering bet that intelligence is computation - specifically goal-directed search and learning over a world regular enough to predict - and that scaling those general methods with compute and data, rather than hand-coding cleverness, is what produces capability. Everything else (ML, deep learning, LLMs, agents) is a mechanism that falls out of that bet.
