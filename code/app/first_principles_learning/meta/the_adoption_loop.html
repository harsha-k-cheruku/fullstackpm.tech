The Adoption Loop

How anything - a product, an idea, a skill, a habit, a relationship - travels from unknown to indispensable. One flow, seven stages, running under commerce, learning, and life. Arguably the first lens to reach for, because almost everything is something being adopted by a mind or a market.

Topic folder: first-principles-tutor/meta · Captured 2026-07-12 · The demand-side mirror of Evolution; ties together Trust, Aggregation, and the tutor method

0TL;DR

  • One universal flow: Discover → Comprehend → Trust → Adopt → Experience → Retain → Advocate - then advocacy feeds new discovery, so it is a loop, not a funnel.
  • It is universal because anything competing to be taken up by a mind or a market must win two scarce things - attention (Discover) and certainty (Trust) - then survive use (Retain) and replicate (Advocate).
  • It is the demand-side mirror of evolution. Ideas/products/habits are replicators; the loop is how they get selected (discover→trust→adopt), retained, and copied (advocate). That is why it feels foundational - it is a consequence of the evolution substrate.
  • It applies to learning exactly as to buying: a concept must be encountered, understood, believed, used, felt to work, remembered, and taught. Skip trust or retention and "learning" is just exposure.
  • Not one canonical framework - a family. It unifies AIDA, the funnel, AARRR, Rogers' Diffusion of Innovations, and the Hook Model. We name the unified version, crediting the priors.
  • The master question at every stage: which stage is the bottleneck? Effort spent on the wrong stage is wasted - you cannot fix a trust problem with more discovery.

1The seven stages

Each stage is a gate with a distinct job. Fail one and the whole thing stalls there - the later stages never get a chance.

  DISCOVER -> COMPREHEND -> TRUST -> ADOPT -> EXPERIENCE -> RETAIN -> ADVOCATE
     ^                                                                    |
     |____________________ advocacy feeds new discovery __________________|
#StageThe job / gateFails when...

The two hardest gates are Trust and Retain

Discovery gets all the attention (marketing, launches), but the flow usually dies at Trust (uncertainty unresolved) or Retain (no habit forms). Those are the expensive, defensible gates - and the two most often under-invested. A thing that is easy to discover but impossible to trust, or delightful once but never sticky, does not compound.

2Why it is universal (the deep reason)

This is not a marketing coincidence. The loop is forced into existence by three deeper facts you have already met:

  • Attention is scarce (from friction / abundance). Anything competing to be taken up must first win attention - hence a Discover gate must exist.
  • The world is uncertain and verification is costly (from Trust & Verification). Before committing, a mind must reduce risk - hence a Trust gate must exist. It is the demand-side face of "verification is the bottleneck."
  • Replicators are selected and retained (from Evolution as the Meta-Algorithm). Whatever survives in the ecosystem of minds/markets is whatever gets selected, retained, and copied.
The Adoption Loop IS evolution, seen from the demand side

Evolution = variation + selection + retention. Map it: the world offers many candidates (variation); Discover→Comprehend→Trust→Adopt is the selection filter a mind/market applies; Retain is literally retention; Advocate is replication (copying the winner into new minds). So the loop is not arbitrary - it is what the evolutionary algorithm looks like when the thing being selected is an idea, product, or habit, and the environment doing the selecting is human attention under uncertainty. That is why it applies to everything: everything valuable is a replicator running this gauntlet.

3It is a loop, not a funnel

The classic "funnel" picture is wrong in one important way: it ends at purchase. The real structure closes - and the feedback is where all the compounding lives.

  • Advocate → Discover. A retained, delighted adopter refers others (word of mouth, Wrapped, a taught student) - which is the cheapest, most trusted form of someone else's Discovery. Retention manufactures new top-of-funnel.
  • Experience → Trust. Each good experience deepens trust, lowering the trust cost of the next adoption (upsell, the next lesson, the next purchase).
  • Retain → switching cost. The longer something is retained, the higher the cost to leave (data, habit, identity) - which protects it against the next competitor's Discover gate.
The flywheel reading

Because the loop feeds itself, a thing that clears all seven gates does not grow linearly - it compounds (this is the network / flywheel effect). The whole game is: get one full turn of the loop, then make each turn cheaper than the last. Aggregators, habits, and great teachers all live on this feedback.

5Applied to learning (your key insight)

You are right that this is the lens for learning - because most "learning" fails silently at a specific gate:

  • Discover - you encounter the concept.
  • Comprehend - you understand it in the moment (the dangerous stage - it feels like learning but nothing has stuck yet).
  • Trust - you actually believe it, having pressure-tested it, rather than passively accepting it. (This is the falsifiability step - what would prove it wrong?)
  • Adopt - you use it deliberately at least once.
  • Experience - you apply it to a real problem and see it work (or find its edges).
  • Retain - it survives forgetting (spaced repetition, revisiting) and becomes a reflex.
  • Advocate - you teach it or weave it into your web of ideas - the Feynman test and the synthesis move. Teaching is the highest-retention act; it closes the loop.
Why most learning dies at Comprehend

Understanding something in the moment (Comprehend) feels like the finish line - but it is only stage 2. Without Trust (you'd defend it), Experience (you've used it), and Retain (it survived a week), you have exposure, not knowledge. This is exactly why the two super-prompts here push you past comprehension: the Tutor forces Trust (pressure-test the axioms) and the Synthesis Partner forces Advocate (connect and teach it). The whole method is engineered to move you through all seven gates, not stop at two.

7Is it an existing framework?

Honestly: domain-by-domain, yes - as a unified cross-domain loop, this is our synthesis. We are not inventing adoption funnels; we are naming the shared skeleton behind a whole family:

What "The Adoption Loop" adds

It is the substrate-independent union of all of the above: all seven gates, framed as a loop, and applied to anything being taken up by a mind or a market - not just products. Crediting the priors is the point; the value is the unification and the "which gate is the bottleneck?" discipline. (Name it whatever you like - Uptake Loop, Discovery-to-Devotion - the structure is what matters.)

8Edge & breaking cases

  • It is not strictly linear. Stages overlap and iterate; trust builds through experience, not only before it. Treat it as gates that must all eventually clear, not a rigid pipeline.
  • Some stages collapse. A reflex/impulse purchase skips Comprehend and Trust; a mandated tool skips Adopt-as-choice; drilled facts skip Trust. The loop still explains why those cases feel shallow or brittle.
  • Coercion / addiction shortcuts it. Monopolies force Adopt without Trust; addictive products force Retain without genuine Experience-value. The loop still runs - it just runs on a hijacked gate (cross-ref mismatch).
  • Apophenia check. The loop earns its universality only where the gates genuinely apply. For a purely one-shot, no-choice event, forcing all seven stages adds nothing - drop it there.

9How to use it

The two-move method

Move 1 - Locate the bottleneck. For anything you are trying to grow (a product, an idea, your own learning, a relationship), ask: which gate is it stuck at? Discovered but not trusted? Adopted but not retained? The bottleneck is almost never where you are spending effort.

Move 2 - Spend only on the bottleneck. You cannot fix a Trust problem with more Discovery (more ads for a distrusted product); you cannot fix a Retain problem with more Adoption (more signups that churn). Diagnose the gate, then invest there - and remember Advocate feeds Discover, so retention is often the cheapest growth.

The one question

For anything you want to take hold - in the market or in your own head - ask: "Which of the seven gates is it failing, right now?" That single diagnosis redirects almost all wasted effort.
