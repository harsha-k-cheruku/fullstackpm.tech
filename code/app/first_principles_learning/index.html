First-Principles Tutor

Become a sharper thinker and builder from first principles. Two wings: Think - understand how the world works, from the irreducible facts up; and Build - the craft of turning that understanding into working systems.

The method used in every note here: separate the axioms (irreducible facts, true even if the subject never existed) from the mechanisms (the consequences that fall out of them). Learn the axioms and you understand why; learn only mechanisms and you just memorize how.
The one flow to internalize first

The Adoption Loop - how anything (a product, an idea, a skill, a habit, a relationship) travels from unknown to indispensable. It is arguably the first lens to reach for, because almost everything is something being taken up by a mind or a market:

Discover → Comprehend → Trust → Adopt → Experience → Retain → Advocate
  ↰ ...and Advocate feeds new Discovery, so it is a loop, not a funnel.

It is the demand-side mirror of evolution (selection → retention → replication), which is why it runs under commerce, learning, and life alike. The master move: ask which gate is the bottleneck - you cannot fix a Trust problem with more Discovery. Open the full note ->

How this collection fits together

This is no longer a single ladder - it is a layered stack. Each layer's mechanisms become the next layer's first principles; a foundation sits below, cross-cutting lenses run through every level, and a meta layer wraps the whole thing. The stack below maps the Think wing; a second Build wing - the craft of making things - follows it.

          [ META - how we know / how to think ]   the lens around it all
  ======================================================
   APPLIED ECONOMY   Human Needs -> Businesses -> Build (+ Abundance)
   CONSEQUENCES      Future of Work · Economics of AI · Cost Collapse
   THE HUMAN LAYER   Psychology · Behavioral Economics (adoption speed)
   THE TECHNOLOGY    AI -> LLMs -> Agents
  ======================================================
   THE SUBSTRATES    Information · Energy · Evolution · Networks · Computation
          [ CROSS-CUTTING - Dynamics + Forces + Trust ]
                 (apply at every layer)

Read it bottom-up or top-down. Bottom-up: the substrates (information, energy) enable the technology, which reshapes work and the economy, all mediated by human psychology. Top-down: what to build (applied) rests on consequences, which rest on the tech, which rests on the substrates.

The meta-principle: the mechanisms that fall out of one layer become the axioms of the next. That is how knowledge compounds - and it is the reusable method behind every note here.

How to read this

The fast path (a week of good reading, top of the stack down):

Mental Models (the on-ramp) -> AI -> LLMs -> Agents -> Future of Work -> Economics of AI -> Human Needs framework + the Patterns capstone -> The Build Playbook.

The deep path (bedrock first, bottom of the stack up):

Information Theory -> Energy -> Evolution -> Networks (the substrates) -> then the tech stack -> then Cost Collapse & Abundance for the big picture -> and Epistemology last, to see the method examine itself.

Cross-cutting, dip in anytime: the Dynamics, Forces, and Trust notes apply to every layer - read them whenever a topic makes you ask "how does this move over time?", "who has the power here?", or "why should I believe this?"
