Energy & Thermodynamics from First Principles

Energy is the master resource. Everything - a bacterium, a thought, a GDP figure, a trained model - is energy being transformed from one form into another. Learn its two laws and you hold the accounting rules for the entire universe.

Topic folder: first-principles-tutor/foundations · Captured 2026-07-12 · Substrate under ../how-tech-changes-the-world/, ../economics-of-ai/, and ../foundations/information_theory.html

0TL;DR

  • Energy is the capacity to do work - to move, heat, build, compute, or think. Nothing happens without a flow of it.
  • 1st law (conservation): energy is never created or destroyed, only transformed. The total is fixed; you can only change its form.
  • 2nd law (entropy): in a closed system disorder always rises. Every transformation leaks some energy as low-grade heat - you cannot break even. This is the arrow of time.
  • Life cheats locally, pays globally: an organism builds order inside itself by exporting more disorder to its surroundings. Order here is bought with chaos over there.
  • Energy is the real currency: money, food, and GDP are proxies for energy flows. The story of civilization is the harnessing of ever-denser energy (fire -> agriculture -> coal -> oil -> nuclear).
  • Even thinking has a fuel bill: erasing information has a hard minimum energy cost (Landauer's limit). Datacenters run hot for the same reason your brain does.
  • The payoff: energy is the binding constraint on AI. The first GPT (cheap electricity) now gates the latest (cheap cognition).

1What energy IS - and the 1st law

Strip away the equations and energy is a bookkeeping quantity: the capacity to do work, where work means causing change against a resistance - lifting a weight, heating a room, pushing electrons through a chip, firing a neuron. If something happened, energy flowed. If nothing can happen, there is no usable energy left.

It wears many costumes - kinetic (motion), potential (position), chemical (bonds), thermal (jiggling atoms), electrical, nuclear, radiant. The deep insight is that these are all the same stuff in different clothes, and it converts freely between them: food (chemical) -> muscle motion (kinetic) -> body heat (thermal).

The first law: conservation

Energy is never created and never destroyed - only transformed from one form to another. The total energy of a closed system is constant. A car engine does not "consume" energy; it converts chemical energy in gasoline into motion and (mostly) heat. The books always balance to the last joule.

This is why "the capacity to do work" is the right definition rather than "a kind of fuel." Energy is not used up - it is degraded, moved from a concentrated, useful form to a diffuse, useless one. The first law says the quantity is fixed. The second law, next, says the quality only ever falls. Hold both at once and almost everything else follows.

2The 2nd law / entropy - the tax on every transformation

If the first law is the accountant, the second law is the tax collector. Entropy is a measure of disorder, or equivalently of how spread out and unusable energy has become. The second law states: in a closed system, entropy always increases (or, at best, stays flat). Order decays. Hot things cool. Structure crumbles. It never spontaneously runs backwards.

Three ways to say the same law

Disorder version: a closed system drifts toward its most disordered, most probable state.

Heat version: heat flows from hot to cold on its own, never cold to hot without paying.

Efficiency version: no transformation is perfect - some energy always escapes as low-grade waste heat.

The practical punchline is brutal and unavoidable:

You cannot win, and you cannot break even

Every time you convert energy from one form to another, a portion is lost as heat too diffuse to do further work. A power plant, an engine, a solar cell, a neuron - all leak. You can chase efficiency but you can never reach 100%. The universe charges a transformation tax, always, and it is non-refundable.

This is also the origin of the arrow of time. The laws of motion look the same run forwards or backwards, but entropy does not: a shattered cup never reassembles, a mixed coffee never unmixes. The direction "future" is simply the direction in which entropy increases. Time has a direction because disorder has a preference.

Energy vs exergy - the quality distinction

The first law counts total energy; but not all joules are equal. Exergy is the useful fraction - the part that can still do work. A bucket of lukewarm water and a small hot coal may hold the same energy, but the coal has far more exergy. Entropy is really the accounting of exergy being destroyed. When people say energy is "consumed," they mean exergy was spent - the quantity stayed, the usefulness left.

3The profound reframe: life is a local entropy-reducer

Here is the puzzle that stops people cold. The second law says everything drifts toward disorder - yet life is exquisitely ordered. A seed becomes a tree; cells build intricate proteins; brains assemble ideas. How does life create order in a universe sworn to destroy it?

Schrodinger's answer: negentropy

In What is Life? (1944) Schrodinger noted that an organism "feeds on negative entropy." Life does not break the second law - it exploits a loophole. A living thing lowers entropy inside itself (order) by pumping more entropy out into its surroundings (waste heat, CO2, excretion). The books still balance in the universe's favor: total entropy rises. Life is a local pocket of order paid for by a global increase in disorder.

The clearest picture is the dissipative structure (Ilya Prigogine): a system that maintains its internal order precisely because energy flows through it. A whirlpool exists only while water drains; a flame's shape holds only while fuel burns; a candle, a hurricane, a cell, a city, an economy - all are ordered patterns that persist by continuously degrading high-quality energy into low-quality heat. Stop the flow and the structure collapses back into equilibrium (which, for a living thing, is called death).

The reframe worth keeping

You are not a thing; you are a process - a standing wave in a river of energy. Order is not free and it is not permanent; it is rented, and the rent is paid continuously in exported entropy. Every structure that matters - a body, a company, a civilization, a trained model - is a dissipative structure holding back the tide only as long as energy keeps flowing through it.

4Energy is the real currency

Because nothing happens without an energy flow, energy is the substrate under every other kind of value. Money, food, and GDP feel like the fundamentals - but they are proxies for energy flows. A calorie is stored solar energy. A dollar is a claim on future work, and work requires energy. GDP tracks energy use almost lockstep because an economy is, physically, a machine for transforming energy into goods, services, and heat.

Civilization = harnessing ever-denser energy

Human history read through this lens is a single escalating story: each leap is the mastery of a denser, more concentrated energy source.

EraEnergy sourceWhat it unlocked
FireBurning woodCooked food (pre-digestion) -> bigger brains, warmth, tools
AgricultureThe sun, via cropsStored solar energy -> surplus, cities, specialization
CoalAncient buried sunlightSteam, industry, the escape from muscle power
OilDenser buried sunlightMobility, plastics, the modern world
NuclearThe atom itselfEnergy density millions of times higher than chemical fuel

Each transition let humans command more work per person, and prosperity followed the energy. But raw energy content is not the whole picture - what matters is the net gain after you subtract the energy spent getting it.

EROEI - energy return on energy invested

You must spend energy to get energy: to drill a well, build a solar panel, mine uranium. EROEI = energy returned / energy invested. Early oil gushers returned ~100:1 - almost free power. As we scrape harder-to-reach sources the ratio falls, and below roughly 5:1 to 7:1 a society struggles to run everything else (hospitals, schools, art) on the leftover surplus. The real question is never "how much energy is there" but "how much surplus is left after the getting."

5Computation costs energy

It is tempting to think information is weightless and thinking is free. It is not. Computation is a physical process, and the second law taxes it like everything else. The deepest result here connects thermodynamics directly to information.

Landauer's limit

Rolf Landauer (1961) proved that erasing one bit of information has a minimum, unavoidable energy cost - it must dump at least kT ln 2 joules of heat into the environment (k = Boltzmann's constant, T = temperature). Logical irreversibility (throwing information away) is physical irreversibility (making heat). You cannot forget for free.

This is why datacenters run hot and why cooling is often their biggest cost. Every logic gate that discards information sheds heat; pack millions of them into a rack running billions of operations per second and you get a furnace. Real chips run many orders of magnitude above the Landauer floor, but the floor is real and it is a wall we are slowly approaching.

The deep link: entropy is the same concept in two fields

This is not a coincidence or a loose metaphor. The entropy of thermodynamics (disorder in energy) and the entropy of information theory (uncertainty in a message, Shannon 1948) are the same mathematical quantity. Information is physical; a bit is a little pocket of order; erasing it pays the same entropy tax as any other transformation. See the companion note Information Theory from First Principles - it approaches the identical mountain from the information side.

6The full circle: energy is the binding constraint on AI

Now the payoff. If life, computation, and civilization are all energy being transformed, then intelligence - biological or artificial - is no exception. A brain burns roughly 20 watts. A large model's training run burns megawatts for weeks. Cognition is not exempt from the fuel bill; it is one of the most energy-hungry transformations we know.

The oldest abundance now gates the newest

The AI explosion is bottlenecked by - electricity. Grid capacity, datacenter power, and the cost of a kilowatt-hour are the hard ceiling on scaling compute. The first General Purpose Technology (cheap electricity) has become the scarcity gating the latest one (cheap cognition). The arrow of progress is not a clean ladder; it loops back and collides with an old constraint. This is exactly the "full circle" from Cost Collapse & the Death of Friction: each abundance unlocks the next frontier, which rediscovers that energy was the real substrate all along.

This is why, in the AI era, serious capital flows into power generation and not only into chips. Whoever controls cheap, dense, reliable energy controls the ceiling on intelligence. The economics of AI are, at bottom, the economics of energy - see Economics of AI. "Intelligence too cheap to meter" is impossible while electricity is not.

7Edge & breaking cases

Jevons paradox - efficiency can increase total use

The intuitive move is: make engines efficient and we will burn less fuel. Reality often does the opposite. When William Jevons studied coal in 1865, more efficient steam engines led to more coal burned, not less - because efficiency made steam power cheaper, which expanded its uses faster than efficiency saved fuel per use.

Jevons paradox

Improving the efficiency with which a resource is used can increase total consumption, because it lowers the effective price and demand rebounds past the savings. Efficient LEDs -> more lighting everywhere. Efficient compute -> more compute everywhere. Do not assume efficiency shrinks the energy budget - it may grow it. This is a feedback loop, unpacked further in Systems Thinking.

Energy cannot be recycled (unlike materials)

Atoms can be reused forever - recycle aluminum, compost carbon, mine landfills. Energy cannot. Once high-quality energy is degraded into diffuse waste heat, it is gone as a usable resource; the exergy is destroyed for good. You can recycle the can but never the joules that shaped it. This is why "renewable" energy is really about renewable capture (of the sun's flow), not recycling.

The heat-death long view

Where the arrow points

Extend the second law to the whole universe and you reach its logical end: given enough time everything degrades to a uniform, lukewarm sameness with no energy gradients left to do any work - the "heat death." No stars, no life, no computation, no thought - just maximal entropy. Every pocket of order we build is a temporary, local defiance of that destiny. Sobering, but also the reason order is precious: it is expensive, borrowed, and against the grain of the cosmos.

8How to use it

This is not just physics trivia - it is a lens you can point at any system, technical or human.

The moves

Follow the energy. To understand any process, trace where the energy comes from, what it is transformed into, and where the waste heat goes. The energy flow is the skeleton under the story.

Ask for the real energy budget. Behind every claim of "free," "unlimited," or "weightless," find the hidden joules. Cloud storage, AI inference, cryptocurrency, a growing city - each has a physical energy bill someone is paying.

Find the EROEI. Do not ask how big a resource is; ask what the net surplus is after the cost of getting it. That surplus is what funds everything else.

Respect the tax. Any plan that assumes a lossless transformation, a free lunch, or perpetual order without inflow is fighting the second law - and the second law is undefeated.
