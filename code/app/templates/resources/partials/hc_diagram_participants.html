Healthcare Participant Map

The US healthcare system is a negotiated network of incentives across patients, providers, payers, PBMs, infrastructure vendors, pharma, and regulators.

Patients / Consumers

End users paying premiums, deductibles, copays, and coinsurance — often with limited control over plan selection.

IndividualsFamiliesEmployer-covered members

Providers

Hospitals, clinics, physicians, and labs deliver care and submit coded claims for reimbursement.

HospitalsPhysiciansLabsPharmacies

Payers (Insurance)

Commercial and government plans adjudicate claims, set reimbursement terms, and manage utilization controls.

Commercial: UHC/Anthem/Cigna/AetnaGovernment: Medicare/Medicaid/VA

Pharmacy Benefit Managers (PBMs)

PBMs manage formulary design and pharmacy claim adjudication, negotiating rebates with manufacturers.

Express ScriptsCVS CaremarkOptumRx

Infrastructure

EHRs, clearinghouses, and standards power data exchange and claims routing across fragmented providers and payers.

EHR: Epic/CernerClaims: Change/AvailityStandards: HL7/FHIR

Pharma & Device Manufacturers + Regulators

Manufacturers set product pricing and rebate contracts; FDA/CMS/HHS/state bodies define safety, reimbursement, and privacy constraints.

Drug & Device MakersFDACMSHHS / State DOI

System reality: no single actor controls the full journey. PMs win by designing workflows that reduce coordination friction across institutions with conflicting incentives.
