Each lending business model has a distinct clearing sequence — different participants, different handoffs, different fee moments. Select a model to trace the flow from application to funded.

How it works: The platform is a matchmaker only. It sells the borrower's lead to multiple lenders and exits. It never underwrites, never holds credit risk, never disburses funds. Revenue is earned per lead sold — regardless of whether the borrower gets a loan.

How it works: The platform originates and underwrites loans, then lists them for investors to fund. The platform never holds loans on its own balance sheet — investors bear the credit risk. Revenue comes from origination fees (upfront, one-time) and servicing fees (ongoing, percentage of outstanding balance).

The platform earns fees from both sides without touching the balance sheet. Three parties must align simultaneously for every loan.

How it works: The bank and the platform are the same entity — fully vertically integrated. The bank funds loans from its own deposit base. No bank partner, no capital marketplace. The bank holds credit risk directly on its balance sheet and earns the full interest spread (loan APR minus deposit rate).

Makes monthly repayment to bank
Receives repayment. Earns interest spread (loan APR minus deposit rate) on every payment for the full term. May cross-sell other products.
Receives interest on their deposits — the return the bank pays for using their capital
Bank earns: Interest spread (loan APR minus deposit rate, held for full loan life) + Origination fee + Cross-sell from existing banking relationships.   |  Risk: Bank holds full credit risk on balance sheet — no external party absorbs defaults.
