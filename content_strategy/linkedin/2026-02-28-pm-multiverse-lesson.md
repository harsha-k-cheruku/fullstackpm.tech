The best way to learn product management is to sit in the disagreement.

Not to hear one "right" answer.
Not to memorize a framework.
Not to read a case study that tells you what the PM "should have done."

But to watch five smart people look at the same problem and reach different conclusions.

And then figure out where you stand.

That's what real PM work feels like. Every day, in every room, with every stakeholder. Someone wants to ship fast. Someone wants more data. Someone wants to bet big on one wedge. Someone wants to derisk everything first.

Nobody's wrong. They're optimizing for different things.

When I built PM Multiverse, I didn't include a "correct answer" screen.

Because there isn't one.

There are trade-offs. There are risks. There are different philosophies about how to build products.

Your job as a PM isn't to find the right answer. It's to understand the trade-offs well enough to make a decision you can defend — and then ship.

Where do you tend to land?

A) Validate first, ship when confident
B) Ship fast, learn from live data
C) Find the sharpest wedge, go all in
D) Something else entirely?

Curious what this community thinks.
