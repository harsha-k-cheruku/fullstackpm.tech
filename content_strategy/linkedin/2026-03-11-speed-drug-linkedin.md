**Speed Drug: What a 19th-Century Economist Tells Us About AI**

Imagine a machine that produces donuts at 100x the rate you can eat them.

It doesn't make you better fed.
It makes you rush through each one without tasting it.
Grab the next before finishing the last.

Technically consuming more. Meaningfully nourishing less.

That's what's happening to AI power users right now.

Here's the cold math:

1) **Google, 2009:** 400ms of added latency reduced daily searches by 8 million. Speed restructures behavior — permanently.
2) **Amazon:** 100ms extra load = 1% revenue loss. The threshold never moves back.
3) **UC Irvine:** After an interruption, deep cognitive work takes 23 minutes to fully resume. An LLM that takes 15 seconds doesn't slow you down — it ends your session.
4) **The ratchet:** Every generation of technology moves the patience threshold. Telegraphs in 1870. Web pages in 1993. AI in 2026. Thresholds don't move back.
5) **The new inequality:** Fast model access → more cognitive output. Throttled access → interrupted thinking. Not information inequality. *Cognitive velocity inequality.*

Every prior fast tool augmented communication.

LLMs augment *thinking itself*.

And the super user's response to AI that's fast enough to outrun reflection isn't to slow down — it's to generate more, faster, and process each thing more shallowly.

There is a version of LLM dependency that produces the output of many people but the wisdom of none.
