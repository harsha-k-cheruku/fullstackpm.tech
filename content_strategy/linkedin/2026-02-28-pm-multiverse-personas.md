## Best posted: Wednesday (build-in-public day)
## Goal: Engagement + shares from PM community

---

I built a PM case study tool with 5 AI personas.

Each one looks at the same problem and reaches a different conclusion.

Here's who they are:

📚 Marty Cagan — "Prove your assumptions before you ship a single line."
Outcome-first. Risk-driven. Won't launch without evidence.

🔭 Teresa Torres — "When's the last time you talked to a user? Not last quarter — last week."
Continuous discovery. Weekly touchpoints. Evidence over opinions.

⚔️ Shreyas Doshi — "Find the sharpest wedge and pour everything into it."
Strategy-first. Ruthless prioritization. Concentrate resources.

📈 Lenny Rachitsky — "Personally recruit the first 100. Then build the referral loop."
Growth loops. Experiments. Benchmarks. Retention obsession.

⚡ The Exec — "Two weeks. Ship it. Learn from live data."
Speed beats perfection. Markets don't wait for your research.

The interesting part: they don't agree.

On YouTube Brazil, Cagan and Torres want to validate creator supply first. Lenny and the Exec want to ship now and learn from live data. Doshi wants to pick one wedge — maybe music — and ignore everything else.

Three camps. One problem. No right answer.

That's the tool. You answer first, see how they'd each handle it, then vote on whose approach you'd actually run.

Doshi wins more votes than I expected. PMs apparently love the "sharpest wedge" philosophy.
